Squat Exercise Variations

The squat rack is one of the most important pieces of equipment for a proper resistance training leg day. Loading up the bar with plates allows you to practice this compound movement to increase leg strength and core stability. Compared to its variations, it is the best exercise for hitting multiple muscle groups without isolation. Safety racks are necessary to perform this somewhat dangerous movement, however, as loading up too much weight can lead to injury very quickly.
The smith machine is a squat rack variation that allows for safer racking of the bar using hooks and clips attached to it. This variation can allow users to reach a deeper movement in their squat while also loading up more weight on the bar with confidence due to the safety hooks. It is a more isolating movement compared to the squat due to this, however, as it does not force your core stability to activate like the normal unassisted squat rack does.
The leg press is a machine with many different variations. The incline seated variation is the one I am most familiar with, and it allows for me to move the greatest amoount of weight compared to the other variations listed on this website. Due to this, it also has the most relative isolation, mainly targeting the glutes and quads with correct form. I would argue that it has the potential to be the safest out of the three variations when using the same amount of weight for each.
